lightbulbThe Idea

The demand for medical equipment is high due to COVID-19 and people are becoming more health aware. I am going to build a cheap, reliable oximeter ( it measures oxygen level in the body) because it is a very essential item these days and finding one is hard right now. I am thinking of driving down the cost through some cost cutting or some technological innovation. I understand that the idea is not new but worth pursuing.

attach_moneyThe Cost

In the initial phases of the business I would like to operate from home to save money on rent. Later from the small capital earned and some number of satisfied customers I can try to find an investment so that I can scale the business. The correct model for cost for me would be: The cost of raw material for me is 7.5 dollars. C(x) = mx (where m is 7.5 dollars)
So my cost function after substituting value of m will be :-
C(x) = 7.5x
where C(x) is cost in dollars.

account_balanceRevenue Function

I have decided to sell the product for 10 dollars. For every increase in price by 0.15 dollars we sell 10 less products. Now let's try to build the revenue function. Let number of price increases be denoted by x. Revenue function be denoted by R(x)
R(x) = ( number of products sold ) * ( price of product )
To find revenue function as a function of x we have to find number of products and price of product as a function of x.
Number of products sold = 500 - 10x ( assuming 500 products are sold in first month)
Price of product = 10 + 0.15x ( price of product initially 10 dollars )
So now substituting number of products and price of product in revenue function we get
R(x) = (500 - 10x) (10 + 0.15x)

We can observe from graph that we get maximum value as 5000 at x = 0

The domain of function is [0, ∞) as the number of price increases cannot be negative practically.
